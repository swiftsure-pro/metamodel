Arguments deal with whether claims are true or not, providing reasons to support that claim and implying that the premises make it reasonable to accept the conclusion. In contrast, an explanation describes the mechanism by which something happens, providing causal theories for why something could happen. Explanations may themselves be true or not (i.e. explain adequately why something happens), but they cannot be used as evidence to believe a claim – they make it plausible, but not certain. <br/><br/><br/>
